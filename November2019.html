Quianna Lynell
 Black Cab Jazz Series
 Music
Nov 9 (Sat) @ 7:00pm

In 2017, Quiana Lynell made her salient mark on the jazz-and-beyond musical world as a wise, heartfelt, warm vocalist whose voice is singular in its soul, intensity, ecstasy, and outright spiritual courage. She played with her trio at the New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival which led to her performing in Poland with trumpeter Terence Blanchard’s Spike Lee tribute with a 75-piece orchestra.

Curated by internationally renowned arts organization San Jose Jazz in partnership with the Hammer Theatre Center, the Black Cab Jazz Series is named after the Hammer's intimate black box performance space, where the concerts are held in the spirit of classic, intimate cabaret shows. This riveting set of up-close performances featured Blue Note Records drummer Kendrick Scott Oracle (Oct. 20) and will feature sizzling vocalist Quiana Lynell (Nov. 8), one of New York’s finest drummers Matt Wilson and his “Honey & Salt” Quintet (Feb. 21), and award-winning saxophonist Melissa Aldana Trio (Mar. 20). El Libro Centroamericano de los Muertos:
Bilingual Reading and Conversation
with Mexican Poet, Balam Rodrigo
Borderlands Series
SJSU @ Hammer
Nov 14 (Thu) @ 3:00pm

Balam Rodrigo, an acclaimed poet and dynamic speaker from Chiapas, Mexico, joins us in November for a bilingual public event at the Hammer Theatre and a day-long creative writing workshop in Spanish on the SJSU campus, co-sponsored by the Chicanx/Latinx Student Success Center.

Rodrigo’s latest award-winning book, El Libro Centroamericano De Los Muertos (2018) focuses on the experiences of Central American immigrants as they cross more than one border in their perilous journey towards the U.S.. The book-length poem incorporates the spiritual cosmovision of indigenous people from the region, while simultaneously exposing the brutal realities of their contemporary flight from violence and poverty. [dreamer] project: an Undocuplay
Borderlands Series
SJSU @ Hammer
Nov 15, 16, 21-23 @ 7:30pm
Nov 24 (Sun) @ 1:00pm

[dreamer] project: an Undocuplay is a verbatim theatre piece created from interviews with SJSU undocumented students. The play focuses on the continuing journeys of undocumented students from Mexico and Central America, and the issues surrounding these young people who seek to find a home in the U.S..

By showing the challenges each face as SJSU students, as well as personal stories of childhood and family, we come to know them not just as “undocumented” but as human beings navigating an extraordinary time in our history.

This is the story of our students. Read more.

What Is The Hammer?
The Hammer Theatre Center is a distinctive, high-quality performance venue in the heart of downtown San José. Operated by San José State University, the theatre serves the university and the community through artistically and educationally excellent programming expressive of the unique characteristics and diverse cultures that comprise Silicon Valley.
